What is "Project PARSNIP"?

Project PARSNIP (otherwise known as PARSNIP) is a community-made project that aims to spread accessible resources about FIRST shirt trading culture. PARSNIP is designed to provide shirt trading guides, accessible shirt trading templates, and more to the public.This project is powered by driven community members! Whether it's sharing Google Sheet templates, photos at meetups, or insights from competitions, your content enriches our community's experience.

THE BASICS OF SHIRT TRADING

Shirt Trading Overview

WHAT IS SHIRT TRADING?
Shirt trading is a popular subculture within the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) community. It involves participants (students and adults alike) exchanging team apparel at events such as regionals, district championships, and most notably at the World Championships. Shirt Trading continues to gain popularity every season, and has become more accessible with the wide use of social media platforms, such as Discord and ChiefDelphi, resulting in trades also by mail.WHY DO PEOPLE TRADE?
Many people have different reasons; some people actively wear FRC apparel every day, some collect for novelty, and some do as a reminder of notable moments within their FIRST career (for example, winning a competition with a specific alliance).WHERE DO MEETUPS HAPPEN?
Meetups can happen anywhere! However the most consistent and notable meetup happens on Friday of the World Championships.HOW CAN I GET INTO SHIRT TRADING?
Trading, itself, is an easy and fun experience that anyone can do! This guide will take you through the essential steps and frequently asked questions.
Generally, trading requires confidence, communication, and negotiation. Shirt trades can happen prior to competition or spontaneously, so being prepared is essential! As collections grow, some of the "rarity" will too. But it's not easy to upgrade to trade up to "rare" status immediately.

STEP 1: RECORD YOUR COLLECTION

Congratulations, you've just taken your first step into shirt collecting!To begin your shirt trading career, it's essential that you first record what team apparel you do have. To do this, it's highly recommended you make a spreadsheet, allowing others to easily access your collection, as well as accessing it anytime through any device. Google Sheets is usually recommended for this, due to their easy UI.When making your sheet, you should include these keypoints:
• Team Name & Number
• Year of Apparel (if applicable)
• Size
• Tradability (how willing you are to trade it)
• Other key commentsIn addition, it's also recommended that you take photos of your individual items. This is for several reasons. To start, it's to help verify that you own that piece of merchandise, as well as the general quality of the apparel. In addition, it's also to help show what specific piece of gear it is (specific competition year, print run, etc.) You can include this as a Google Drive link to your spreadsheet.FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Q: Does it only have to be shirts? What if I want to trade hoodies or something else?
A: It can be any form of apparel! While shirts are the most common, many people trade for hoodies, sweatshirts, hats, pants, socks, etc.Q: I don't have apparel! What about buttons, pins, stickers, wristbands, etc.?
A: While not common, trades for "competition wares" exist! If it exists on someone's spreadsheet, they're probably interested in trading for it. Please note that not everyone will be interested in trading for it if they don't explicitly mention it.Q: How do you make a fancy spreadsheet?
A: check out our video guides below for in-depth tutorials on Google Sheets.

STEP 2: TRADING TIME

After recording your collection, trades can start happening! Trades can happen anywhere and on any platform. This section will briefly cover the different avenues of shirt trading.

Trades are most likely to happen in person. This is to ensure immediate exchange, limited inventory being carried, and so much more.

The World Championships are the most prominent form of shirt trading, as many in-person trades happen here between two parties, present or not. Here is a list of tips of how this may differ from normal in-person trading:


  • Proxy Traders: sometimes there will be people trading who are not physically present. These people rely on someone else, who will bring their collection to the Championships and trade on the person's behalf. Some people have say in every trade, while some others will give others a set and have little say.

  • Select Days/Times: in addition to trades, which may happen spontaneously or reoccurring, the World Championships hosts an annual shirt trading meetup. This usually happens the Friday of the World Championships, and lasts throughout the entire lunch period (generally 12pm to 3pm).

    • Outside of the main season, the off season sees many trades as well! Online resources such as Chief Delphi and the FRC Shirt Trading discord have several members who host shirt trades and auctions.

    TRADING ETTIQUETE

    Resource submitted by RJ (Mentor) on 5406• Don’t interrupt a team from what they’re focusing on! If there’s a match going on or if their robot is being worked on in the pits, let them focus on that. Ask them afterwards.
    • Don’t take someone else’s trade! If trade talks are in progress, don’t suddenly step in without permission.
    • Don’t prevent a trade from happening!
    • Don’t be afraid to ask! The worst answer you can get is a no. If you get a no, don’t constantly ask every other member of the team you’re interested in.
    • Be respectful when declining a trade. If you aren’t interested, thank them for their interest, ask for their info, and ask if they are interested in putting their request online. if so, direct them to Chief Delphi or the FRC Shirt Trading discord.    Please remember that you're not only representing yourself, but your team and the team's merch you wear (if different from your team).

    STEP 3: BUILDING UP YOUR COLLECTION

    With the basics of shirt trading down, it's now time to build your collection. There's many things to keep in mind when building your collection:1. BUILDING A LARGE COLLECTION TAKES TIME, EFFORT, AND PATIENCE. Great collections don't usually come overnight.
    2. There are multiple ways to grow your collection -- auctions, purchases, fundraisers, trades, and so much more!
    3. Collecting is more about your collection; its about the fun of making new connections and wearing cool merch!
    4. Everyone has different values for merchandise. This is usually indicated on their sheet. If you are curious about a trade, it never hurts to ask.
    5. Sometimes items may be rare because of their limited quality. However, that doesn't mean all scarce merchandise is valued rare.
